Ronda Nicole Mitchell believed in the power of education. She pursued her degree not as a formality, but as a statement — a declaration that she was capable, determined, and worthy of every opportunity that knowledge could open. In 2015, she earned her Associate of Science degree in Computer and Information Sciences and Support Services from Athens Technical College, becoming a proud member of both the Alpha Beta Gamma International Business Honor Society and the Phi Theta Kappa Honor Society.

She was the kind of student who made those around her better. Her curiosity was contagious, her commitment to learning ran deep, and her natural brilliance for technology — the ability to disassemble a computer and rebuild it from scratch — was something she carried long before it had a degree attached to it. STEM was not just a field of study for Ronda. It was the language her mind naturally spoke.

"She could take apart a computer and rebuild it like second nature. Her love for technology was a reflection of how her mind worked — precise, curious, and always solving."

In her memory, the Athens Technical College Foundation is accepting donations to the Foundation Scholarship Fund — a fund that supports students who, like Ronda, are working to build a better future through education. Every contribution made in her name carries forward the spirit she lived: sharp, determined, and driven by love for learning.

If Ronda's life meant something to you — if her intelligence, her warmth, or her persistence touched you in any way — consider honoring her by investing in a student who is walking a path she would have recognized and admired.
